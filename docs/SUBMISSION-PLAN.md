# submission plan

## strategy

submit to a 中科院2区 journal while securing ALife community visibility via a conflict-free late-breaking abstract.

## step 1: journal submission (this week)

submit full paper to **Royal Society Interface**.

- 中科院分区: 2区
- IF: ~3.5
- APC: free (Cambridge read-and-publish agreement with Royal Society, extended through 2028)
- scope: "cross-disciplinary research at the interface between the physical and life sciences" (our exact pitch)
- review: ~10 weeks
- format: no strict page limit; 12 pages, 8 figures accepted as-is
- action needed: reformat from CUP template to Royal Society LaTeX template, write cover letter

## step 2: ALife 2026 visibility (by 20 July)

submit 2-page **late-breaking abstract** to ALife 2026.

- deadline: 20 July 2026
- format: max 2 pages, poster presentation
- proceedings: none (no DOI, no formal publication)
- conflict with journal: none (this is the safe path)
- conference: 17-21 August 2026, Waterloo, Canada (hybrid)

do NOT submit a Full Paper to ALife (deadline 30 March). Full Papers are MIT Press publications with DOIs, which would create a prior-publication conflict with the journal submission.

## step 3: adapt based on journal outcome

- if Royal Society Interface **accepts** before Aug 17: present at ALife as a Summary (2-page track for already-published work, automatically accepted into programme)
- if Royal Society Interface **rejects**: immediately redirect to iScience (Cell Press), then present at ALife as late-breaking abstract
